# Upgrade Prompts to Bring Each Cover Toward 9/10

## Universal prompt to combine with any selected cover image

Use the attached cover-art background as the exact visual basis. Preserve the same overall composition, aspect ratio, series language, negative-space logic, and blank wraparound function. Do not add text, typography, barcode, spine guides, mockup lines, publisher marks, or labels. Refine it into a more print-ready, vectorizable literary book-jacket background: cleaner edges, fewer muddy micro-textures, stronger silhouette hierarchy, controlled spot-color palette, and a clearly usable title reserve on the right/front-cover side. Preserve the cream paper ground, the modernist small-press atmosphere, the bird/branch motif, and the sense that this belongs to a unified four-volume John Gould Fletcher edition. Reduce busy detail by roughly 20 percent while increasing symbolic force. Make the result elegant, severe, printer-friendly, and suitable for a dust-jacket wraparound.

## Volume I — The Early Works

Use the attached Volume I black-branch cover as the basis. Preserve the cream ground, black tree, birds, and austere apprentice-symbolist mood. Improve it by making the branchwork more deliberate and less noisy, keeping the left/back-cover side visually rich while leaving the right/front-cover side open for title typography. Add no scenery and no color beyond black and warm cream, except the faintest paper texture. The mood should be early Fletcher: nature, dusk, apprenticeship, and latent modernism. Make the silhouette more elegant and spine-safe, with fewer stray speckles and a clearer wraparound rhythm.

## Volume II — The Dominant Works

Use the attached Volume II coastal high-modernist image as the basis. Preserve the blue trunks, sea, Mediterranean/French-Riviera poster energy, and the series branch/bird motif. Make it more high modernist and print-safe: flatter colors, sharper blocks, less scenic detail, fewer tiny buildings, and clearer large shapes. Keep the palette disciplined: cream, dark green, cobalt/Prussian blue, pale blue, muted olive, warm ochre, and minimal red-orange roof accents. The image should feel like Imagism, radiance, Japanese-print compression, and modernist confidence. Keep a large clean reserve on the right/front-cover side for typography.

## Volume III — The Embattled Works

Use the attached Volume III rock/sea/airplane image as the basis. Preserve the angry biplane, angular black granite, hostile sea, breaker forms, thorn-branch motif, and severe negative space. Improve the composition by making the aircraft slightly more legible and purposeful without making it cartoonish or militarized. Keep the rock mass heavy on the left/back-cover side and the right/front-cover side open enough for title type. Use a reduced palette: cream, black, charcoal, slate blue, dull ochre, and small oxidized-red stress marks. Make the whole image more Vorticist, prophetic, and print-safe, with clean geometry and fewer noisy textures.

## Volume IV — The Falling Works

Use the attached Volume IV Ouachita/Arkansas valley image as the basis. Preserve the dark ridges, winding river, South Star, bird in the sky on the right, and sparse black tree at the left. Make it simpler, more Arkansas-specific, and more terminal: fewer scenic details, more low Ouachita ridge energy, broader negative sky, flatter land forms, and a quieter elegiac mood. Keep two birds in the left tree if possible and one bird in the right sky. Palette should stay restrained: cream, black, deep olive, dark umber, muted ochre, restrained blue-gray river, and one small burnt-orange accent. The final effect should be Arkansas reduced to myth: river, ridge, star, branch, mountain, silence.
