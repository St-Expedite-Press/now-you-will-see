## Original Lineation

I contemplate my toil,
    Fruit of the dead years done:
A vast unreadable coil,
Heavy as leaden stone.

This sodden lifeless weight
    Scrawled over all with black:
To do this was my fate?
    Better to serve the rack!

Dreaming to toss my song
    Into the roaring skies,
I broke my back with wrong,
    And paralysed my eyes.

So now I ask but rest.
    The dream of long ago
Is broken: on my breast
    Bury my poems, too.

## Context Notes

Four quatrains in ABAB ballad form.

l. 18 ('Better to serve the rack!'): 'Rack' is ambiguous between a storage frame (absurdly mundane) and the torture instrument (darkly hyperbolic); either reading intensifies the rejection of the accumulated creative work.

l. 28 ('Bury my poems, too'): The poems are treated as part of the body to be interred with it, not preserved as a monument.
