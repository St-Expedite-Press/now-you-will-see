## Original Lineation

I am alone, and my little light
Yearns for the immensity of the night:
Yet though it surrounds me, I cannot attain
To its formless joy, free from change, free from pain;
To its everlasting linked dust of the stars
Entangling my little fears and cares:
To its deep vanity, of which I, a part,
Am become a symbol of perverse art.

I yearn and I burn and I would not sleep:
But thought holds me ever imprisoned deep,
While I move, restless, and without sight,
'Mid the vague deceits of our mortal night.
O weary of dreams, to the sky I pray:--
May my eyes see that darkness which is day!

## Context Notes

Fourteen lines in AABBCCDD EEFFGG, the form of a modified couplet-sonnet rather than a Petrarchan or Shakespearean division.

ll. 1–2 ('I am alone, and my little light / Yearns for the immensity of the night'): A paradox: the individual consciousness desires dissolution into the darkness it is surrounded by, rather than increase.

l. 14 ('May my eyes see that darkness which is day!'): The closing prayer draws on the *via negativa* tradition (negative theology of Pseudo-Dionysius the Areopagite): darkness beyond ordinary perception as a higher illumination.
