## Original Lineation

Vestal withered and unkissed,
Raphael with rheumatic wrist,
Beautiful garment on an ape:
Such is my poor body's shape.

Orator who rants alone,
Sisyphus rolling still his stone,
Sunlight shining on the blind:
Such shall be, through life, my mind.

Titan fighting with a louse,
Caesar keeping herds of cows,
Runner starting at the goal:
Such as these is still my soul.

Emperor of desert sands,
Don Juan in Boreal lands,
Penniless Iscariot:
Such, at last, must be my lot.

## Context Notes

Four quatrains in AAAB CCCB EEEB GGGB, each stanza building three parallel self-comparisons concluded by a single summary line.

The poem's structure of paired impossibilities -- greatness degraded by a fatal flaw -- echoes Baudelaire's 'L'Héautontimorouménos' (*Les Fleurs du Mal* LXXXIII): 'Je suis la plaie et le couteau' (I am the wound and the knife). Fletcher's instances are bathetic by design: Raphael with a rheumatic wrist, Caesar tending cows, Don Juan in the Arctic.
