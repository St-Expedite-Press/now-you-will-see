The fifth and final 1913 collection — Fletcher's most Symbolist and Decadent book. The opening poem is addressed to Baudelaire's memory; the collection includes translations or adaptations from Japanese hokku, Baudelaire, and other sources. It is the shortest and most compressed of the five books. Notable context notes: Baudelaire four ways — *Le Crépuscule du matin* (V 001 area), *Les Litanies de Satan* (V 001), *L'Héautontimorouménos* (V 013), *L'Albatros* (V 014); J. D. Fergusson (V 023); hokku adaptations dated against the Imagist association (V 024; originals an open research task).
