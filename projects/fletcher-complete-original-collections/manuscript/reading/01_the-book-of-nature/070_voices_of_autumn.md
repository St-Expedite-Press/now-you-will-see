## Original Lineation

The rain spattered the pond,
  The wind shook at the trees:
But late, in the hours beyond,
  The sun came, bringing peace.

Peace to the sodden walks,
  Peace to the shivering trees,
Peace to the withered stalks;
  Everywhere there was peace.

## Context Notes

A compressed eight-line lyric in ABCB quatrains, almost a coda: after the elaborate elegies and apostrophes of the Versailles sequence, the poem presents a simple moment of post-storm peace. The anaphora ('Peace to the...') recalls the liturgical form of the benediction or blessing, and the four-fold repetition of 'peace' achieves through sheer repetition what theological language achieves through ceremony. Set still in the Versailles gardens ('sodden walks,' 'shivering trees,' 'withered stalks'), the poem is the briefest and most transparent in the Versailles section.

Form: two quatrains of three-beat lines rhymed abab, and both times the b-rhyme is 'trees' set against 'peace' -- the word the poem says four times until it is so.
