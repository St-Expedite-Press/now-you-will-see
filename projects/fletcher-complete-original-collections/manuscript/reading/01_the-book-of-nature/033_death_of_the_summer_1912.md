## Original Lineation

She lieth dead, her dark hair all astream,
  A silver shape wrapt in soft folds of rain.
And from her grey eyes faded is the dream
  Of that dim peace which asks not joy or pain.

## Context Notes

A four-line poem that personifies the summer of 1912 as a dead woman -- 'a silver shape wrapt in soft folds of rain,' with 'dark hair all astream' -- in the elegiac tradition of Swinburne, who habitually feminized nature and natural forces. The date '1912' in the title is an autobiographical marker, anchoring the poem in Fletcher's London period and making the poem a brief memorial to a specific disappointing year. The quatrain form (ABAB) with its exact rhyme (stream / dream) is the compressed mourning-verse of the epitaph tradition, paired with the poem that follows: 'Epitaph on the Summer of 1912.'

Form: a single elegiac quatrain -- iambic pentameter, abab.
