## Original Lineation

I fed my soul on vast green towering trees
    Beneath which roses made eternal June,
And, lured by drowsy song of drunken bees,
  I fell asleep and spent long years aswoon.

Then from wild peaks, wine-red in day's decline,
    I drank; and still the acrid taste remains
Of all that ever-flowing endless line
  Of scanty, sun-parched, aromatic plains.

I clad myself in all the shimmering veils
  Of sound which breezes weave in upland pines:
Or the harsh clash of waves which, sped by gales,
  Upon a flat shore crash in foaming lines.

I warmed myself before the terrible fires
    Of life, which roar in many a city vast:
Before all pains and joys of man's desires,
  His dubious future and his tragic past.

And then I roused myself in stubborn will
  And, fenced with pride which baffles woe and wrong,
I fashioned images joyous, terrible.

  And sent them forth to walk on feet of song.

They come to where, beneath the green trees' shade,
  Bloom roses of June, dream-passionate and sweet.
From other songs and worlds all unafraid
  They come, to kneel as slaves before your feet.

## Context Notes

Form: six elegiac quatrains -- iambic pentameter rhymed abab. The source print breaks the fifth stanza's last line ('And sent them forth to walk on feet of song.') off as a stray; the relineation above restores it to its quatrain.

The initials are best read as Fletcher's sisters: A.F.T. is Adolphine Fletcher Terry (Terry by marriage from 1910), M.F. her sister Mary. Read so, the closing turn -- songs sent 'to kneel as slaves before your feet' -- is a brother's homage despatched home to Little Rock while the books themselves were bought into print in London. The identification rests on the initials and should be confirmed against a biography before production.
