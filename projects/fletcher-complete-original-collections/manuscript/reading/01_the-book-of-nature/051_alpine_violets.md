## Original Lineation

(Imitated from Du Bellay)

You tiny flames of blue,
Whose naive barbaric hue
  On grey rock-slope,
Seem like the sky let through
A painted window new,
  Grant me your hope!

I weave thin coronets,
Slim madrigalettes,
  Loose coronals of rhyme,
For you, whose blossoming
Turns summer into spring,
  Defeating Time.

Where 'gainst the frozen snows,
Rosy blue, each one glows
  Amid sparse grass:
Like young girls with tossed hair
Ring-dancing; O, from there
  I would not pass!

## Context Notes

The subtitle's Du Bellay is Joachim du Bellay of the Pleiade. The 'slim madrigalettes' and the ring-dancing girls are his Renaissance furniture, carried up and transplanted above the snow-line.

Form: three tail-rhyme sixains -- long couplet, short tail, twice over (aabccb; the first stanza doubles its rhyme, aabaab). The tail-rhyme chanson is the right French furniture for the imitation the note above identifies.
