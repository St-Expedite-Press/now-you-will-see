## Original Lineation

To the bees' resounding hum
  Lo, they come!
First, upon the bare hillside
Rosy spikes, the almond's pride;
  Piercing with empurpled glow
  Windswept dark-blue skies that show
  Far-off faint peaks white with snow:
Next the cherry from which song
Shakes the white shower all day long,
  Ringing to stir the sluggish growth
  Of a thousand others bursting forth
On the glowing green hillside,
Which becomes a mosaic pied,
  A vast galaxy of hues
  Whose perfume breezes catch and lose.
Later, peach pours forth her breath:
Dandelions powder the grass beneath.
  Day's too short to count each head
  Green or golden, blue or red:
'Mid the life-flushed rout I see
Asphodels that sway in glee,
Spring's mad immortality!

## Context Notes

A single unbroken catalogue of spring blossoms: almond, cherry, peach, dandelion, asphodel, each named in turn. The asphodel in the final line — 'Spring's mad immortality!' — shifts the poem's register abruptly: asphodel is the flower of the classical underworld (Homer's Plain of Asphodel), and its appearance here as a sign of spring's immortality rather than death gives the catalogue its sting. Twenty-one octosyllabic lines in couplets with two triplets ('glow/show/snow' at ll. 15–17, 'see/glee/immortality' at ll. 20–22); the source prints it as a single stanza and no relineation has been imposed.
