## Original Lineation

I fled from blustering March
To where the heavens arch
  With blue of summer day:
  March caught me on the way.
Sudden he blew his horn;
Straight was the sky uptorn:
  Northward he shook his locks,
  Southward he smote the rocks;
Sped by in pelting sleet,
With winds were shod his feet,
  A cloud his charioteer
  Over the shivering mere . . .

O could I be a wind on a March day,
  Filled with pure joy of an unconscious being,
  I would arise and hasten onward, seeing
Whereto the month of storms hurries away.

## Context Notes

The poem splits between two formal registers: twelve rapid couplet-lines dramatize March's passage ('Sped by in pelting sleet, / With winds were shod his feet'), then a pentameter quatrain breaks off at an ellipsis and ends with the wish to be the wind itself — a pantheistic impulse that recurs across *The Book of Nature*. The couplets run; the quatrain watches them go.

The editorial relineation breaks the first section into three four-line groups at ll. 5 and 9 without altering any text.
