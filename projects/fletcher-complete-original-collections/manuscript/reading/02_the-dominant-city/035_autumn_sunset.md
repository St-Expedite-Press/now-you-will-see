## Original Lineation

Evening and the clear sun
Slides down opal ways;
London like a crystal
Shines beneath my gaze.

Rosy, pearly, blue and brown,
Is the pale-washed sky;
Right and left, and up and down,
Gleaming roof-tops lie;

In the calm of autumn
All the city seems
A young giant dreaming
Fair and foolish dreams.

## Context Notes

'Fair and foolish dreams' (l. 12): the qualification 'foolish' deflates London's Olympian scale — the city is a young giant, but its dreaming is not tragic or heroic, just foolish. Three quatrains of three-beat lines, abcb (the middle stanza tightening to abab).
