## Original Lineation

Motionless blood-hued styluses that scrawl on
  the infinite
Shifting surface of sky for ever and ever unrolled,
Man's greatest achievement and failure, through
  infuriate day and through night,
In hieroglyphs rolling and tumbling, red, black,
  purple and gold.

## Editorial Relineation

Motionless blood-hued styluses that scrawl on the infinite
Shifting surface of sky for ever and ever unrolled,
Man's greatest achievement and failure, through infuriate day and through night,
In hieroglyphs rolling and tumbling, red, black, purple and gold.

## Context Notes

A stylus is a pointed writing instrument for inscribing wax tablets or engraving; a hieroglyph is an Egyptian sacred writing character, used here for any indecipherable inscription. The chimneys write hieroglyphs of smoke on the infinite sky, making industrial pollution an act of illegible text. 'Infuriate' (l. 3) is used as an adjective in an archaic literary sense: causing fury, maddening. A single quatrain of long anapestic lines, rhymed abab.
