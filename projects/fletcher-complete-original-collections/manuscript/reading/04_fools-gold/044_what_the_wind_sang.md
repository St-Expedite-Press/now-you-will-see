## Original Lineation

The broad trees shaken together in a great wind
Roar and murmur, laughing: "It is life, it is life":
The nodding flowers and grasses up the hillside
Sing together, steadily: "Birth and love":
But by the pond the little slender sedges
Low and thrilling whisper: "It is death."

## Context Notes

Six unrhymed lines in a single stanza, the three voices structured in diminishing order: trees (roaring) → flowers and grasses (singing) → sedges (whispering). l. 6 ('sedges'): grass-like plants of the genus *Carex*, growing in wet, marginal habitats.
