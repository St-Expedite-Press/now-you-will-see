## Original Lineation

I am maddened with wine and sorrow,
I am scorched with love's fiery breath:
And now a whisper runs through me,
"Hasten, for I am Death."

I desire many new songs to fashion,
Songs for the spirits of men:
But ever that shudder repels me:
And there is no singing, then!

I dream of the world rolling headlong
Down ages, with my hand to steer:
But all that I see is--that phantom!
Yes, he is here!

I have toiled: I have tasted men's pleasures:
I have breathed with their common breath.
And now there is only one song in my heart--
The iron pulse of death.

## Context Notes

Four ballad quatrains, ABCB throughout. The first three stanzas each introduce an aspiration cut short by Death's intruding voice (ll. 4, 8 — the refrain-like interruption); the fourth stanza resolves into acceptance without interruption.
