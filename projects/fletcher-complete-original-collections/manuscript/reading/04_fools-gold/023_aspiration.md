## Original Lineation

Stars, stars above, glittering, glittering, glittering;
Stars every clear night in the same place on high:
Stars afar, very old you are, yet deathless:
Are you the spheres I must go to when I die?

Stars of my soul, that gaze in my eyeballs,
Strange silent companions steadily signalling,
Some day perchance among your flame-walled cities
I shall pass, while earth moves on from spring to
        spring!

What fresh adventures shall I find among you?
What joys, what loves, what strange and dreadful
        wrong?
What new sorrows will assail my spirit,
Filling it full with the wild revolt of song?

Stars, stars above, glittering, glittering, glittering;
Stars every clear night in the same place on high:
Stars afar, very old you are, yet deathless:
Are you the spheres I must go to when I die?

## Context Notes

Four quatrains with a refrain structure: the opening quatrain (ll. 1–4) repeats verbatim as the closing quatrain (ll. 28–31). ll. 19–20 and 23–24 are turnover lines in the source; the editorial text restores full lines.
