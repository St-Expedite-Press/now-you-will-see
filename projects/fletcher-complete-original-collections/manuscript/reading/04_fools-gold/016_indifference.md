## Original Lineation

No more the crested wave of joy for me,
The gloomy trough no more:
Smooth, bare, and glittering is the endless sea
Without a glimpse of shore.

On the horizon dim and far away
Becalmed lie many sail;
I watch them passing through the arid day,
While I await the gale.

I am a mariner without a chart,
Or any coast to find;
A merchant with no cargo for the mart,
I steer a voyage blind.

My sails are rent and ribboned, and my mast
Leans crazily askew.
Nothing that floats on brine but has surpassed
My speed, as if it flew;

Meanwhile my keel is rotten, for 'tis old,
Gapes open to that wave,
Sunlit but grey, burning but bitter-cold,
My master and my grave.

Must I yet strive to keep afloat above
The appalling, unplumbed deep,
Which never lights with any glow of love
And never wakes from sleep?

Some day I'll grant the waters' utmost will,
Open a breach to sea,
Of bitterness unchanging take my fill,
And break the mystery.

## Context Notes

Seven cross-rhyme quatrains, ABAB throughout. The poem sustains a single maritime metaphor across all seven stanzas. l. 37 ('The appalling, unplumbed deep'): 'unplumbed' — not yet measured by the lead-line; depth unknown.
