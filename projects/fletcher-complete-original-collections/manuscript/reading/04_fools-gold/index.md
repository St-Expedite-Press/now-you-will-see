The fourth 1913 collection — Fletcher's most philosophically ambitious early book. Its poems are organized as a sequence of abstract nouns (*Hope*, *Ambition*, *Drunkenness*, *Discontent*, *Failure*, *Life*, *Freedom*, *Knowledge*, *Sorrow*, *Loneliness*, *Attainment*, *Faith*, *Blasphemy*, *Indifference*, *Sin*, and so on), each poem meditating on one quality or state. The prelude and the poems in the sequence maintain a consistent meditative-philosophical register. Notable context notes: Ecclesiastes (FG 037), Blake's *Tyger* (FG 015), Villon via Rossetti (FG 039 vicinity), *Contradiction* crux (FG 002).
