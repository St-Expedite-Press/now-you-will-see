## Original Lineation

There is not a thing to fear
In the wide world round us here:
First we are born, and then we die,
Without knowing the reason why:

Between these two noteworthy events
We may climb to omnipotence,
Or sink downwards to the pit
Without losing the joy of it.

As long as hope remains deceiving,
So long we firmly cling to living:
And grimly fight for one last breath,
Anything being better than death.

Courage we do not need at all;
Were there no death, we should soon fade out;
There is not a thing to fear,
Nor a thing to be brave about.

## Context Notes

Four cross-rhyme quatrains, ABAB throughout. The argument moves from a general proposition (l. 1, 'There is not a thing to fear') to a paradox: ll. 26–27 ('Were there no death, we should soon fade out') — mortality as the condition of vitality.
