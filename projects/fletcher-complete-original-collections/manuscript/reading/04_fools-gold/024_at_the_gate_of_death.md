## Original Lineation

Dying to live, I have only lived to die,
A poor bedraggled mock of men, my brothers;
Now my heart shaken spills its agony
In thick, slow drops; now the vague twilight
        gathers
Upon the motionless tree-tops standing high
Within the cemetery of my hopes:
Darkness creeps slowly down drear upward slopes
That lift into the chill and ashen sky.

No soul has climbed with me life's stony hill:
No soul has joined me at the wayside rest:
No soul companions me, now death must fill
With horror and anguish my breath-struggling
        breast:
I have suffered in solitude from now, until
This moment: now once more I cry in vain
My utmost pulsing agony of pain:
No word, no echo: earth and air are still.

## Context Notes

Two octets rhyming ABBAABBA / CDCDCDCD — a double Petrarchan octet rather than a sonnet. l. 1 ('Dying to live, I have only lived to die') is a chiasmus. ll. 14–15 and 24–25 contain turnover lines in the source; the editorial text restores full lines.
