## Original Lineation

Truth is so beautiful, I would not stir
To take her: 'tis apart I worship her.
Beauty is more than all the things to be,
I seek her ever: she despises me.
Justice is like the lightning from the sky,
It strikes the great oak first--I would not die.

Were real things as my dream, there were no dream
To veil in glorious lies Time's iron stream:
To make The Commonplace arise and dance,
And blind Fate's tearless eyes to all but Chance.

## Context Notes

Ten lines in five rhyming couplets (AA BB CC DD EE), divided into two stanzas of six and four lines.
