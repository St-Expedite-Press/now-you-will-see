## Original Lineation

I was offered the wine;
I refused it--the fool!
Now I drink bitter brine
In solitude's school.

When kisses were offered
I waved them away,
Who once spurns the proffered
May nevermore play!

In vain was I called
To Youth's table of bliss;
Now I fast on the salt
Of sharp loneliness.

Afar from a port,
I must wander to test
The storm-gusts that snort
On the sea's savage breast.

Years have fallen like leaves,
Age is creeping more near;
He whom no hope reprieves,
What fate need he fear?

Not a glimmer of light
On the black scene of care!
I'm alone with the night,
And alone with despair!

Let howl the cold sea;
Let the storm fill my soul;
Let the rudder bang free,
And the night be my goal.

## Context Notes

Seven ballad quatrains, ABCB throughout. The poem divides into two movements: social regret (ll. 1–12) and a maritime extended metaphor (ll. 13–28). l. 43 ('Let the rudder bang free'): a rudder without a helmsman is a precise figure for voluntary aimlessness.
