## Original Lineation

Stars great and lonely,
Let me be one of you,
To burn myself to ashes in the night.

Pain, hate, and solitude have made
A banal tragedy, ill-played,
Where I am audience and actors, too:

I taste each moment the bitter poison of life,
The irony of time,
The foolishness of fate!

All of my effort is worn out:
Let me again a child become,
That I may taste this old despair anew.

Stars burn blue and gold:
I rush, a falling star,
Downwards, from the night into the night.

## Context Notes

Five irregular free-verse stanzas. The poem opens (ll. 1–3) and closes (ll. 27–29) with a stellar address, framing the personal statement between cosmic images.
