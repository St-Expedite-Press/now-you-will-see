A critical reading edition of Fletcher's five books published in 1913 — his first sustained body of work, written during a period of self-financed expatriate publication before his association with the Imagist movement. The edition is arranged in order of publication and separated into five books, each with its own internal organization.

Editorial work: C. Sandbatch. Established 2026.

## Books

- [The Book of Nature (1910–1912)](01_the-book-of-nature/index.md) — 74 poems
- [The Dominant City (1911–1912)](02_the-dominant-city/index.md) — 44 poems
- [Fire and Wine](03_fire-and-wine/index.md) — 51 poems
- [Fool's Gold](04_fools-gold/index.md) — 67 poems
- [Visions of the Evening](05_visions-of-the-evening/index.md) — 40 poems

## Structure of Each Poem File

Each poem file carries up to three sections:

1. `## Original Lineation` — always present; verbatim from the transcription witness
2. `## Editorial Relineation` — only when the editor has made line/stanza decisions
3. `## Context Notes` — only when a decision required justification
