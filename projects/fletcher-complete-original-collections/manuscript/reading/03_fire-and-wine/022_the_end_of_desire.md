## Original Lineation

All my dreams have brought me pain,
  All my hopes have fallen to dust:
Happiness I strove to gain,
  All I got was fresh disgust.

Every pleasure has been brief,
  Granted but to tempt anew;
And the strife they cost, the grief,
  Now too late I learn to rue.

Through the wilderness of years
  I have followed vain desire.
Slowly fall the rain's cold tears:
  It is night. Put out the fire.

## Context Notes

Three ABAB quatrains in alternating tetrameter, a nihilistic recantation of desire. ll. 9–10 ('Through the wilderness of years / I have followed vain desire'): the Exodus narrative — forty years in the wilderness following a mirage — with exhaustion where the promised land should be.

l. 12 ('It is night. Put out the fire'): commands the extinguishing of the erotic fire that is also the collection's first title (*Fire and Wine*). The poem closes Book I by performing its own ending.
