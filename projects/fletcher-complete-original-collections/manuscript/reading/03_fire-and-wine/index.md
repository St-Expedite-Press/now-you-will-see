The third 1913 collection, divided into two books: *Book I: Fire* (love poems) and *Book II: Wine* (ars poetica poems). The love sequence follows a seasonal arc from spring through winter; the ars poetica sequence meditates on the poet's vocation, the public's indifference, and the nature of artistic ambition. The collection is more formally regular than the nature or city books, with a preference for quatrains and sonnet-adjacent forms. Notable cross-references: the Summons/Poet's-Autumn variant pair (B1 035 + FW 034, cross-noted); Rimbaud's *Voyelles* and Bakst (FW 025); Catullus 85 (FW 029).
