## Original Lineation

I am the smith of words, lord of cold torrents of
  tone
That like flags tossed to and fro, undulate wide
  from my lips.
A fluid mosaic, each letter a luminous stone,
  A stalactite grotto, all over which the light drips.

I am the anvil of moods: slave of the fulminate
  world
That, like a prismatic kaleidoscope, leaps instan-
  taneously through
My soul: flamboyant flamingoes 'gainst torpid
  tortoises hurled:
Chrome corollas confused with corallas chrysoprase-
  blue.

I am the iron of thought, the sword of singing
  enchased.
Let the dark clouds roll northward, the blood-red
  sunset pass!
I see aloft in the sky green gargoyles interlaced
  Leering or scowling or dreaming from cathedrals
    of steel and of glass.

## Editorial Relineation

I am the smith of words, lord of cold torrents of tone
That like flags tossed to and fro, undulate wide from my lips.
A fluid mosaic, each letter a luminous stone,
  A stalactite grotto, all over which the light drips.

I am the anvil of moods: slave of the fulminate world
That, like a prismatic kaleidoscope, leaps instantaneously through
My soul: flamboyant flamingoes 'gainst torpid tortoises hurled:
Chrome corollas confused with corallas chrysoprase-blue.

I am the iron of thought, the sword of singing enchased.
Let the dark clouds roll northward, the blood-red sunset pass!
I see aloft in the sky green gargoyles interlaced
  Leering or scowling or dreaming from cathedrals of steel and of glass.

## Context Notes

Three twelve-syllable quatrains, each opening 'I am the...' -- a proclamatory anaphora that places the poem in the tradition of prophetic self-definition (compare Rimbaud's 'Je est un autre' and Whitman's catalytic 'I' in *Song of Myself*). The three stanzas enact the poem's title: smith/anvil/iron marks the three transformations of the creative process (raw material → shaping force → finished weapon). 'Stalactite grotto, all over which the light drips' is a synesthetic image drawn from the mineral world; 'chrysoprase-blue' is a specific pale-green to apple-green chalcedony gemstone, here used as a colour adjective in the manner of Gautier's colour-poems and the Parnassian school's insistence on precise visual equivalents. 'Fulminate' means explosive or thundering, from Latin *fulmen* (thunderbolt). 'Enchased' (also 'enchased') means engraved or inlaid with decorative patterns, a goldsmith's term. 'Green gargoyles interlaced / Leering or scowling or dreaming from cathedrals of steel and of glass' projects medieval grotesques onto the modern city's iron-and-glass architecture -- a characteristic Decadent move of reading the Gothic into industrial modernity.
