## Original Lineation

Sing, O ye poets, sing on,
  Of golden summer's gales;
Of patented magic casements,
  And copyright nightingales!
'Gainst all these harmless follies
  I do not stir up strife,
I am only weary of two things,
  And these are--death and life.

## Context Notes

'Patented magic casements, and copyright nightingales' -- the casements and the nightingales are Keats's, out of the 'Ode to a Nightingale'; the patents and copyrights are the satire. The joke requires the reader to hold the borrowed property in mind.
