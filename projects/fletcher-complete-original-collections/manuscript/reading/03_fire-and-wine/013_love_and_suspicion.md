## Original Lineation

You squeeze me in your arms the while your eyes
  Hungrily seek, fierce woman, on my face
  Close-hid deceits, brief mocking smiles, to trace:
For you have guessed that you have lost your prize.

But I care not, for all my enterprise
  Is sending such thoughts through your brain
    achase;
For thereby do I gain the crowning grace
Of all your splendid savage witcheries.

Seize me and keep me ever for your own,
  So that my love flames in you like a star:
But if you dim its ray, be sure some one
  Shall lure me from your side, and out afar:
This shadowy terror I set on you now,
Leaves but love's perfect lustre on your brow!

## Context Notes

Petrarchan sonnet (ABBA ABBA CDC DCD). The poem presents erotic suspicion as the deliberate fuel of love: the speaker provokes the beloved's jealousy to intensify her attachment.

l. 8 ('witcheries'): enchantments, seductive arts — the lover who knows his power enhances it by the threat of departure (ll. 9–12). The amoral psychology belongs to the Cavalier lyric tradition (Suckling, Rochester).
