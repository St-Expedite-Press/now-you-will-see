## Original Lineation

Ask not a purpose of all things,
  No dogma with fixed mind debate,
But loose each mood to beat its wings
  Freely against the bars of fate.

Welcome each sorrow with full heart,
  As freely as you welcome bliss;
Never to flinch is the best art,
  And to receive all, giving is.

## Context Notes

Eight lines in four rhyming couplets (ABAB CDCD), the poem's title nods to Verlaine's famous 'Art poétique' (1884), which urged poets to pursue music, nuance, and mood over fixed statement -- 'De la musique avant toute chose.' Fletcher's poem shares the anti-dogmatic impulse: 'Ask not a purpose of all things, / No dogma with fixed mind debate.' The instruction to 'loose each mood to beat its wings / Freely against the bars of fate' echoes the Symbolist emphasis on subjective sensation over objective doctrine. 'Never to flinch is the best art, / And to receive all, giving is' rephrases the Keatsian 'negative capability' -- the capacity to remain in uncertainty without irritably grasping after resolution -- as an ethical as well as aesthetic principle.
