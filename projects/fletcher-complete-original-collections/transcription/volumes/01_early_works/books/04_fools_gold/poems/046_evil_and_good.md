# Evil and Good

Long, long they fighting stood,
Evil and good:
Now the great war is done,
Each has the other slain,
Life, their fair prize, again
Stands in the sun.

Over the future deep
Floats now a song:
"Wake, ages, from your sleep
Time's hounds, give tongue:
On to the chase of life!
Nothing is wrong.

"Like to earth, air, and sea,
Let man be free:
Choose as you freely will,
Take either good or ill,
Death's blessing to the weak,
Life's to the strong!"

Nothing's wrong?  Can I do
No evil, then?
Can no sin give me pain?
Can I not feel
To some old god untrue,
To an illusion false?
Silence, you tempting calls!
Bring back the past again!

I must create anew
Evil and good:
Puppets they are, 'tis true,
But I am bored:
Follies I long to do,
Some risk to take--
I might as well be
Cold as a snake.

Come to the battle, then!
Here is my all,
Backing Good's radiant brow:
If he do fall,
Straight to the other side
I will depart.
Are you quite ready?
The bell, then: we start!
