# To Those Who Fail

All sculpture, every painting, every song,
Though it be weak or strong,
Cries but a single message to my mind:
"A dream I sought, a dream I could not find."

Out of the bonds of flesh man's soul desires to leap;
But anchored, grappled deep,
Bears yet the tedium of reality
For a vision wholly vain and but half free.

You lofty souls who fail,
Who win no fame nor joy from out this life,
But only dark days, nights devoid of rest,
Look at the favoured great!  Among them all the
        best
Know only a barren toil, a futile strife,
They plow the sands, and tell a twice-told tale.

Soon will the earth grow cold:
Soon, soon, beneath the mould
Will your bones fall to dust;
And your works will fade and rust.
But when at last life, hope, and time are full,
Your lovely dream will live, all unapproachable!
