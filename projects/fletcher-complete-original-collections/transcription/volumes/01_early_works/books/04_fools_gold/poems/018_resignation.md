# Resignation

I ask you not, O soul of mine,
To be content, for that is vain:
I only ask you to resign
Yourself to pain:--

To make the daily count of rhymes
No goal, but only a poor means
Whereby you waste the last of Time's
Unchanging scenes.

I ask you, bear the nothingness
Of effort, even as earth is hurled
Against the night which grows no less,
Despite our world.

Think of the million others who
In darkness came, to darkness passed;
Who, hopeless, toiled; nor deem that you
Must be the last.

Think of the endless ages gone,
The work made vain, the floods of tears
That altered nothing, and be one
With all those years:

Think of the fate that burdens all,
And for an end that none can tell:
And if with all the rest you fall
Complain you well?

Be silent.  Bear your baleful weight
Like a poor porter-slave in chains
Who strives, unquestioning of fate,
While strength remains!

Since grey must be the brightest light
Of sun within this world unblest,
In the vain struggle spend your might,
And creep to rest!
