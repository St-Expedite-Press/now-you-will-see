# To Death

O Death, slow groundswell of my thoughts' wild
  tide,
Again and again your cold fascination returns:
Again and again, rejecting all things that are,
Vainly I cast myself on the hollow void.

O Death, yon lonely god of rites abhorred,
For you I wait, being a lonely man:
I have spent my life in vain prayer at your gates,
Regretting not, nor feeling any sorrow,
If even so in my death I may pray in vain for life.
