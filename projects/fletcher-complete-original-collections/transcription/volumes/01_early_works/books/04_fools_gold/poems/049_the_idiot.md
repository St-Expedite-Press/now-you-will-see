# The Idiot

I am London's idiot, see!
I live, though my brain has ceased to be:
A better fate than many a head
Which suffers, though its slave be dead:

Your London's made of bricks and coal:
My London grows within my soul.
Your London's grey, both day and night:
My London's yellow, pink, and white.

Within my body ever, God
Insatiably seeks His food:
Walls of my heart, hold firm and strong,
For He would make you yield to song.

God always dies upon the Cross:
Weary of gain, He longs for loss.
God always rises to die again:
Weary of loss, He longs for gain.

He is an Idiot, grey and grim,
He laughs, and I echo Him;
He weeps and I know His woe,
For He is Truth and Falsehood too.
