# The Rocket Ascending

O stars, you who rush through the night,
Look at me now in my flight!
Is there among you one
So loftily, carelessly thrown?
Strewing such mad sparks behind
As it rushes up on the wind?
Vast stars, I know you are great,
More constant than mine is your fate:
But I think you have never borne
The swift change from death to the strife
Of fire-breathing life,
Nor the rush as on burning wings
Out of mortal to deathless things,
From the earth to the skies forlorn.

So, if I fall at the last,
If the struggle is soon done and past,
Think not that I know despair.
You move free from time, space, and care:
I have assaulted the air.
More than you dared, did I dare.
