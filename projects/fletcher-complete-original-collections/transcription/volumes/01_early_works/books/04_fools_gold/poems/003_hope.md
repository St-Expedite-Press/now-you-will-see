# Hope

Hope flashes on some men
While in the dark they grope;
It blinds them to their pain:
To me all things are hope.

Hope will not let me rest;
But like some dawn of storms
Buffets me, as to test
My spirit's strength in arms.

Hope lures my leaping pride
Some steeper crag to scale,
And like a silent guide
Keeps every counsel well.

Hope, letting me not grow
Gross, cheap, and well-at-ease,
Urges my soul to go
Where new thoughts burn and freeze.

Hope, like a lash of steel,
Scars my back every day
With many a well-aimed wheal:
Hope leaves me weak and grey.

Hope, for myself alone,
And through myself, to all:
A sword for those who groan,
A crown for those who fall.
