# Ennui

Bored with all earthly things am I:
With men-machines that grind
Through long grey hours of misery,
Yet leave no trace behind;

Existence stirs up barren strife,
Though it be nothing worth;
Gold is the key that unlocks life,
And Art is done to death.

Yet still I want I know not what,
And sing, but for what reason,
I cannot tell: with me, I fear,
'Tis ever silly season!
