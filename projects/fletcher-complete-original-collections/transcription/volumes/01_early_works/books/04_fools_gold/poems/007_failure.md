# Failure

In all things have I failed:
And all I planned to do
Has come to nothing, too;
In naught have I prevailed.

All has illusion proved,
But will to bear the weight
Whate'er it be, of fate,
And to endure unmoved.

To think I could have been
Anything else but low
Is folly, and I know
I am most weak and mean;

Yet there are some who sigh
At my unchanged despair,
And whisper softly, "There
But for God's grace go I."

No honour then your due!
If God did all, why then
You are the least of men:
Him do I thank, not you;

Your mockeries on me cast;
You mock a moment long!
But I keep sound and strong
My dream until the last:

My soul it is, my breath;
A fool, I shall not sell,
But leave it in my will
A last bequest to Death.
