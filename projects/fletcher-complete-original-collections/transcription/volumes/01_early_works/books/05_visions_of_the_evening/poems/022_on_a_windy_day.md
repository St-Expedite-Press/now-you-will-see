# On a Windy Day

My heart is drunken with an old dance of
    swords,
Played by black pipers in innumerable
    hordes,
At dawning of a flapping, wringing day,
Where the red rowan flares upon the grey.
My heart is drunken with an old dance of swords.

My heart is maddened with a wild dance of war;
With keen blades flickering, flickering afar:
And desperate flights heartbreaking, chill, and sore,
Over the treeless, houseless, puddled moor:
In the mist there go click of swords and cries of
    war:--
My heart is broken by that old dance of war.

My heart is weary of a black dance of swords:
Far off there skirl the shrieking pipes, the hordes
Of pipers vanish, far off over the snow.
O, I am weary, weary, of dull woe:
I would have done with sorrow and with words.
My heart is weary of a black dance of swords.
