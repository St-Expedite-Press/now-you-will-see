# I Contemplate My Toil

I contemplate my toil,
    Fruit of the dead years done:
A vast unreadable coil,
Heavy as leaden stone.

This sodden lifeless weight
    Scrawled over all with black:
To do this was my fate?
    Better to serve the rack!

Dreaming to toss my song
    Into the roaring skies,
I broke my back with wrong,
    And paralysed my eyes.

So now I ask but rest.
    The dream of long ago
Is broken: on my breast
    Bury my poems, too.
