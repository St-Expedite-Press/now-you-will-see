# Dead Thoughts

As flowers smell sweeter, having been wet by
    the rain,
So my thoughts pour forth in the damp
still night their song:
And the clean fresh perfume of their inextinguish-
    able pain,
Seems to pass from the earth and to suffer no
    wrong.

And my thoughts are of mouldering poems, left half-
    unexpressed,
Desires which I felt long ago, I know not why:
They are crumbled to dust like flowers a long while
    pressed,
Yet they still preserve the faint sweetness of love
    gone by.

Green gardens they filled: now they fall one by one,
Upon the dark roads that lead ever outward far:
The flowers of youth, green-white as a frozen star,
The flowers of sorrow, dead ere their spring was
    done.
