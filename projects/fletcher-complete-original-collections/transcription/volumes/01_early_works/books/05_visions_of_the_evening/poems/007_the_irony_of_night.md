# The Irony of Night

'Gainst the sorrows of to-day,
    I have grimly striven to fight,
Waiting for the summons gay
    Of the lamplit streets to-night.

Arches festooned over dark
    Led me onwards to the light:
'Twas a dull and chilly spark
    Closed within a cage of night.

Better was the day but past:
    Then I failed but strove in might:
Now I bind my own strength fast
    To illusions of the night.

'Tis a mockery to sleep,
    And a weariness to fight.
Let the dark days hide me deep
    In the all-encompassing night.
