# Mystic Union

I taste the sultry beauty of your mouth:
  'Tis like a scarlet poppy in the sun.
  Its sleepy passion has my strength undone,
And I am lost in vague dreams of the South.

The scented soft dark tangle of your hair
  Is like the shade of sighing cypress thrown
  On marble terraces where, one by one,
Gold glints are aimlessly drifting, here and there.

Yet in my soul I feel a vague disquiet:
  A lofty melancholy in the sun.
Stilled are my heartbeats, stilled my pulses' riot,
  I dream. Are we apart or are we one?
