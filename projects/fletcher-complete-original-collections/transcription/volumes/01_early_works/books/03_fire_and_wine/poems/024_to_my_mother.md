# To My Mother

Your body bore me: it has mouldered long,
  I, like your dust, am blown along the wind.
  Dead dust of ruin, striving life to find
In frozen temples, made of chiselled song!

Your brain gave mine the light: I bear it yet.
  It flickers palely through the long dark rains
  On the steep road of human wrongs and pains;
It flickers, that my tears may fall in it.

Your soul has passed: it rests aloof, apart,
  Inhabiting freely cool dark realms of space.
  It knows me not: yet from it, by your grace,
I draw the song, the mad song of my art.
