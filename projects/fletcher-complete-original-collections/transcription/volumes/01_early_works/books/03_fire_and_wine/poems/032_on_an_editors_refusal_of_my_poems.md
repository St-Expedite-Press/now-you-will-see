# On an Editor's Refusal of My Poems

Who knows in acid-bitten bronze to scrape
  His thought he need not fear to die unknown:
His immortality he cannot 'scape;
  He rests, as rests the steel-grey granite stone.

Resisting shallow change, resisting death,
  His song pours ever into earth unheard;
The great sigh of the myriads taking breath,
  He knows it his: and that is his reward.

Not his to count the chink of coins, or worse,
  To waste his time in remedying Fame's wrong;
For all the wild force of the universe
  Bursts from his soul, shaking his strings to song.
