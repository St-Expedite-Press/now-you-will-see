# Faith

O God, beyond my spirit's uttermost dreams,
Beyond the most intuitive glimpse of art,
Beyond all whirling, vague, mist-shrouded streams,
Of Consciousness--but yet in me--Thou art.

I am a trial-shape to test Thy worth:
A slight wave-bubble, hastily snatched away,
Thou may'st destroy my paltry chance of earth,
But yet, O God, I thank Thee for to-day.

Wretched and loathsome are man's sufferings,
Weak is my struggle with the encompassing dark:
But yet within my heart a strange joy sings,
And man yet fiercely cherishes life's poor spark.

O God, who hast created me in might
Faith to proclaim, strong faith in life and death:
Although there fall on me the sword of night,
I praise Thee, in my proudly vehement faith.
