# Vanity of Vanities

Life is the pitiless progress from the womb
Unto the grave that closes all our care;
A cage of flesh, a dark and dusty tomb
Where aspiration sinks beneath despair.
A growing grey in body, soul, and mind:
A growing vile in spirit, flesh, and thought:
A lash that speeds us 'round a circle blind:
A bond we cannot 'scape, a bond unsought.

Work is the only means by which we live:
A servitude that earth has fixed us in,
A strife for food to live and further strive,
A punishment for sin, itself a sin.
A balance-sheet in life's bankruptcy filed
Where, on the credit side, stands "Wages due";
But on the other, 'mid the debts up-piled,
"Item: one life lent: I cannot renew."

Love is the body's vengeance on the soul:
A debt contracted for before our birth,
A dark pitfall before a shining goal,
It dulls our days and makes our dreams grow mirth,
For when we seek this phantom, as of old,
Blind to the treachery of ages past,
We fall, and there is nothing but the cold
And silent laughter of the void at last.

Art?  But what then is the bright dream of art?
Only the hope discredited of a heart
That broke when it saw heaven, hell, and earth
To be alike vile and of little worth.

It is a madman's poisoned, dangerous dream:
An imbecile's delight, an idler's gain:
A chance of gold to some with least of strife:
To most, an idle sacrifice of life.

Progress?  'Tis the new-fashioned Purgatory-stair.
On it we climb and climb, yet never see
Within the blue the fountains' ecstasy
Of Paradise: it is a sermon rare
Of good rewarded, preached about a fire
Mid-winter nights, when all is cold and still;
When feeling's numb and dead is our desire,
And we can only strive through conscious will.

God is the curious chance of fate that made
The saint's grief and the devil's joyous leer.
Woe conscious stays, but joys unconscious fade:
Darkness is more each day and more each year.
Old age and pain and death for all our woes
He gives: a blissful sleep, a waking drear,
Although His purposes do not appear,
He knows about it all?  Who knows He knows?

Success is nothing more than being held
By others' efforts higher to the sun.
It is a good life and a bitter one,
Since once for all the illusion is dispelled
That anything you do can ever rise
By what you gave it: though you flame desire,
Your ego falls from out the vacuous skies
Until it grovels, scoundrel, in the mire!

Idleness is a chance for evil thought:
It is an opportunity for truth
To offer you her favours all unsought;
And spurn them as you will and show no ruth,
The horrible hag returns to the attack.
She shows how loathesomely all things are done:
She touches you and makes your body black:
She racks your soul: she muck-defiles the sun!

Death is of all these things the crowning jest.
A merchant, whom we offer suffering,
Joy, hope, dreams, all our heart-beats blest, unblest,
Wealth, fame and life: yea, our poor lives we bring
To him, so he may sell one drop of bliss
Which fades not, but endures; and, while we wait,
Kneeling to him, each one expecting this,
He knocks our heads in with his bludgeon great.

Joy is the vanity of vanities,
Our grotesque crowing-over our defeat:
A snare for all the foolish and the wise,
A mockery to all men, bitter-sweet.
We watch folk laughing at us, while our eyes
Bleed in dumb pain; but when we laugh as well,
Turning the joke upon them, then the prize
Of vanity is ours, and they taste hell!

So all things here are vain, even vanity!
And vanity is vainest of them all.
And as they were, and are, they must still be,
Until the worlds break and the stars do fall.

There is nothing new in vanity under the sun:
Though we should choose, or turn us from the
        strife
It matters not, for all things must go on
Till death slays death and vanity and life.
