# England

I wander far from lands of life;
I part from friendly smiling men:
Within my heart rages a strife
Not to return--but all in vain.

For I am poisoned with the taint
Of a sick, fat, and squalid land;
Why need I rage against restraint?
I wear its very brand.

I come back to a vast, dull place
Where automatic mannikins,
Each with the self-same stupid face,
Pursue their trivial sins.

Their brains with smoke are dry and salt;
They chatter phrases, sweet and trite:
No feelings ever made them halt
Before a hate 'gainst man and light:

They think as think all other men;
No one this custom dare deny:
He who does otherwise is slain,
Or set a laughing-stock on high.

They buy all things from birth to death,
And with that gold Bull which they bear
They turn to slaves men's souls and breath,
Yes, touch the earth and leave it bare.

From servitude's life-lusty mass
They, bloodless, suck the strength away;
Brewing their ancient poison-gas
That hides their doom another day.

Sick, silly, sleepy, they move on
Through the grey suns that rise and fall;
They do but that which must be done,
And one grave hides them all!
