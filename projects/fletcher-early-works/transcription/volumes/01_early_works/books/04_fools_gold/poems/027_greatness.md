# Greatness

If I am great at all, 'tis not because
I have more gold or health than other men,
Or know more joy, or more adventure gain,
Or keep more proudly virtue's lofty laws.

I am the very least of earthly things
Where all these are in question, for I know
Only the least of things may lightly go
Over the gulf on beating, whirring wings.

But I am great, and greater will abide,
Because I do not reck of gain or loss:
I prize all things, and naught to me is dross,
I dare the treachery of every tide.
