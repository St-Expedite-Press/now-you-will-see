# The Mother's Thought of Her Son

He will be kissing women
When I am old;
And he will betray a maiden
When I am cold.

His father was proud and fickle,
I yielded to his guile;
I faced the scorn of women
A long and weary while:

But silent grows your gossip,
For the case is altered now!
The hands that beat upon my breasts,
The lips they yielded to,

Shall plunder of your fairest,
And break your daughters' will.
So I revenge my anguish,
And so I triumph still:

With evil for evil taken,
An eye for every eye,
Contempt for your conventions,
Insult for injury!

And did you think, you women,
That I was weak as you?
Through conquering seed and fibre
My blood's exulting now!

He will be kissing women
When I am old;
And he will betray a maiden
When I am cold!
