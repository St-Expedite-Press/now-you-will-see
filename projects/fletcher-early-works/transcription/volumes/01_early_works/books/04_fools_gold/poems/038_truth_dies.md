# "Truth Dies--"

Truth long has left her well
In the forest dark and cool:
She walks the city now,
Known to each knave and fool.

One pitying her, poor girl,
Furtively wipes a stain--
One slaps her on the back,
One leads her on a chain.

Her fresh and rosy blush
No longer shames the fiend:
For she can blush no more,
Her cheeks with rouge are stained.

And she is bought and sold
Like meat upon a stall;
"Progress" is the false coin
That she is bought withal.

Truth walks the city now;
She whom no man could find,
And no one dared deny,
Is seen--by all the blind.
