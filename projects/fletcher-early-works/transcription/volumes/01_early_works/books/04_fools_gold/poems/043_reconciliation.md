# Reconciliation

Already autumn!  I had hoped at least
To keep ungnawed some moments of this year,
To save some fruit of song for autumn's feast:
But now I know that vain was this my care.

It matters little, if I further go:
Day is but altered twilight, joy half pain,
And frost must ripen fruit.  Let it be so:
I shall not fight the battle over again.

With the dead leaves of all my old despairs,
I'll dance a wedding-round before I rest.
The wind will pipe me mad monotonous airs
Until the night comes, the black night at last.
