# My Confession

O the fools, the miserable footling fools,
The silly sentimental clan:
Who run to hide from naked life,
Who fear to face a man:
I am not now one of your lot,
Although I once began.

The Pharisee thanks God, at least,
For one thing in his life:
You who were bidden to the feast,
Complain of the noise and strife;
And in the back of the Wedding Guest
You slyly slip your knife.

I read my poems of long ago,
And my gorge rose in my throat.
It is to you such songs I owe;
That pretty, piping note,
That nevermore from me shall flow
For fools to doat and gloat.

Thank God, I am cured; and I did not rush
To print my teething pains!
Let me hide my good new light 'neath a bush:
Well out of your sloppy rains.
Someone may come to it from the crush,
If a living man remains.
