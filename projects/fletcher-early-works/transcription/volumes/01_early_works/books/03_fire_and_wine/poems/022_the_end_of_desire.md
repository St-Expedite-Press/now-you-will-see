# The End of Desire

All my dreams have brought me pain,
  All my hopes have fallen to dust:
Happiness I strove to gain,
  All I got was fresh disgust.

Every pleasure has been brief,
  Granted but to tempt anew;
And the strife they cost, the grief,
  Now too late I learn to rue.

Through the wilderness of years
  I have followed vain desire.
Slowly fall the rain's cold tears:
  It is night. Put out the fire.
