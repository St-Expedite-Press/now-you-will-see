# The Poet, I

I am a poet.--Dusky image wrought
After long years within the fires of thought:
Leaving my ancient dross to feed the mire,
And offering up my pure gold to the fire.

Alike to those who tread my vestiture,
  And those who wipe its stain, I am serene:
  I know the meaning of each strange, wild scene;
And every chasm cross with footstep sure.

I am a poet and a mystery,
Each day myself as in a glass I see:
Creator and created fused in one,
Sun that makes night and night that drinks the sun.
