# Autumnal Love

At last I reach with weary strife
  And endless wrestling with the gale,
The autumnal harbour of my life;
  And in that harbour yours the sail.

I am too old, at last, to care;
  It is the end: be this my home,
Where I may watch the red leaves flare
  Before they fall and rot to loam.

Safe by the hearth, I shall forget
  The intolerable loneliness I knew
Out there amidst the sea's wild fret:
  That dread and glorious sea of blue.
