# Lines Written at Stonehenge

Across the low-curved billows grey
  That sweep in swell and hollow round
This summit, lights and shadows play
  In gold and blue upon the ground.
Dark russet groves in the hollows are:
But else, the earth is free and bare.

A sound of far-off cattle-bells;
  A skylark's broken burst of song;
A pearl-white cloud that slowly swells
  To centaur-shape: and, far along,
The rustling of an idle breeze!
So has it been for centuries!

And lo! this summit which no man
  For ages has inhabited!--
And here the dark trilithons stand--
  The relics of what Titans dead?
No human hand could dare to raise
Such temples to the green God's praise.
