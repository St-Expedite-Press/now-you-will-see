# The Flocks of Pan

We are the great God's flocks
Browsing amid the rocks:
  Where olives, a straggling line,
  Wind upwards to his shrine.
There, set on a golden seat,
  Hairy Pan guards us well;
The waves with their rippling wheat,
  The rocks with their asphodel.
            Pan! Pan!

On the crags above deep river-valleys
We drowse in the sun, while the galleys
  Flash past in purple and gold
  To the blue sea below our fold.
The God, half asleep is he;
  And of Syrinx, perhaps, is his dream:
  For the reeds by the bed of the stream
Murmur and sigh restlessly,
            'Pan! Pan!'
