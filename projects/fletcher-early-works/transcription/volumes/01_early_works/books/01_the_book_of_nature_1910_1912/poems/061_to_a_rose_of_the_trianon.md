# To a Rose of the Trianon

Tell me, O exquisite rose that abidest
Here, 'mid the relics of a beauty faded,
From what dust hast thou gathered that proud
  essence
Of perfume and colour that thou giv'st to the
  world?
Thou that hast sucked the heat of the rain-washed
  marbles,
Tremulously flushing with crimson in the sunlight,
Careless thou bloomest, rose of love unaltered,
Though all men's hate and pride about thee fall in
  ruin--
As though all the ages were not--as though the
  world were made new.
