## Original Lineation

Voices heard afar in the wood
  Hollowly echo into my ear;
Voices that seek for something lost,
  Voices that ask but obtain no reply.
'Have I lost or gained?  Did I live or die?'
  Such is the burden they seem to bear.
But time is a wild bee buzzing by,
  And deaf to the voices in the air.

## Context Notes

A brief eight-line lyric set in the Versailles bosquets (the formal groves of trees within the gardens). The 'hollow' voices asking 'Have I lost or gained? Did I live or die?' are either the speaker's own unresolved memories or the ghosts of those who walked these paths under the ancien régime. 'Time is a wild bee buzzing by, / And deaf to the voices in the air' -- the personification of time as a bee too busy with its own trajectory to hear human questioning is a folk-metaphor commonplace, rendered here with unusual economy.

Form: a single octave in four-beat falling lines, the first half barely rhymed, the second locking into alternation ('die/bear/by/air') as the question takes hold.
