## Original Lineation

I too in Arcadia was--how many months ago?
Time has woven new mystery, beauty is faded
  now:
The hours like the jets of fountains upwards and
  outwards flow.

Then all the birds sang daylong, making a merry
  carouse;
Now a late bird cries plaintively from sighing and
  sobbing boughs.
Then the fountain sparkled with jewels: now no
  more the water flows.

Then I was young: there was dancing and sing-
  ing, too, in my heart.
Now I am old and silent: I voyage alone and
  apart,
Towards midwinter sunset, through chill dead
  leaves of art.

My song is as vain as the wind is: but Nature,
  for my sake,
Will bury me deep in the frozen earth, where I
  may never wake
By unfinished marble fountains that Time has
  forgotten to take.

## Editorial Relineation

I too in Arcadia was--how many months ago?
Time has woven new mystery, beauty is faded now:
The hours like the jets of fountains upwards and outwards flow.

Then all the birds sang daylong, making a merry carouse;
Now a late bird cries plaintively from sighing and sobbing boughs.
Then the fountain sparkled with jewels: now no more the water flows.

Then I was young: there was dancing and singing, too, in my heart.
Now I am old and silent: I voyage alone and apart,
Towards midwinter sunset, through chill dead leaves of art.

My song is as vain as the wind is: but Nature, for my sake,
Will bury me deep in the frozen earth, where I may never wake
By unfinished marble fountains that Time has forgotten to take.

## Context Notes

The second of two poems titled 'By the Fountain' in this part. The opening line turns the tomb-inscription 'Et in Arcadia ego' into a past tense: not death announcing itself inside Arcadia, but the poet announcing he has already left it.

Form: four monorhymed tercets in long seven-beat lines, 'heart/apart/art' and 'sake/wake/take' clean, the first two stanzas slanting. The triple rhyme falls and falls again, like the fountain the poem has turned off.
