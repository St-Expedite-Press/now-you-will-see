Fletcher's first published collection, issued in 1913, gathers poems written between 1910 and 1912 during his travels through Italy and France. The book is organized in two parts: Part I, *The Months in Italy*, follows a seasonal calendar from January through December with a series of named month-poems set in the Italian landscape; Part II contains the remaining lyrics, many also rooted in Mediterranean and European travel. The collection's prevailing mode is Romantic-Symbolist: it draws on Keats, Shelley, and the French Parnassians, and shows the influence of classical landscape and seasonal elegy.
