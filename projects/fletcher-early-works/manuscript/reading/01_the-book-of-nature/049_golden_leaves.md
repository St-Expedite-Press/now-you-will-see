## Original Lineation

The dead leaves drift on the walks;
  The dead leaves litter the pond;
They float down like mad butterflies,
  Golden hordes to the southward bound.

On the trees in gold clusters they hang:
  On the turf in gold carpets they rest.
The gold of the year swept away
  Into pawnbroker Time's old chest.

## Context Notes

An eight-line lyric whose governing conceit is leaves-as-currency: 'The gold of the year swept away / Into pawnbroker Time's old chest.' 'Pawnbroker Time' is a witty personification that reduces the great Romantic theme of time's destruction to a commercial transaction -- the year's beauty not simply destroyed but hocked to a pawnbroker for whatever cash it will fetch. The 'mad butterflies, / Golden hordes to the southward bound' are falling leaves compared to migrating insects, a precise visual description of the wavering fall of October leaves. The short tetrameter quatrains (ABCB) with their domestic simplicity contrast with the grander metaphor in the second stanza, and the poem's compression is notable.

Form: two quatrains of anapestic three-beat lines, rhymed abcb ('pond/bound' slant).
