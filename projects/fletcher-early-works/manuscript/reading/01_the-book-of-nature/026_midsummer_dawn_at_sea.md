## Original Lineation

The ship glides steadily on,
As over a sea of glass.
Birds pass -- and then, birds pass:
Horizons melt in one.

The smooth broad flank of earth
Lies sleeping under the sky;
To want, is nothing worth:
To take, is worship high.

Here ships like clouds of white,
There clouds like ships that soar.
Afar the great waves smite
Some broad sun-dazzled shore:
But here like liquid light
They rest upon the floor;
For the gates of morning bright
Open, to close no more.

## Context Notes

The poem dissolves its boundaries at every level: sea and sky, ships and clouds, wanting and having. Ll. 9–10 ('To want, is nothing worth: / To take, is worship high') is a compressed apophthegm in the Nietzschean will-assertion mode. The final image — 'The gates of morning bright / Open, to close no more' (ll. 15–16) — treats dawn as a permanent eschatological threshold, though whether it is death or vision is left open. Two quatrains and an octave in three-beat lines; the octave holds two rhymes only for eight lines, carrying the dissolution to the end.
