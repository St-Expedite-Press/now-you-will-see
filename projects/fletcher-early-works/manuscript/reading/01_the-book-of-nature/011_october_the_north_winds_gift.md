## Original Lineation

Wind, wind, southward scurrying
  What in the north are you fleeing from?
'From the mountains shrouded in darkness
  Round the summer's iron-barred tomb.'

Wind, wind, darkly whirling,
  What is your purpose? Where do you go?
'To the valleys dim and sombre,
  There to scatter, scatter the snow.'

Wind, wind, stony-hearted,
  You take the green, what do you leave?
'Passionate tears for flowers departed:
  Vain wrath, remorse, and icy grief.'

## Context Notes

A question-and-answer ballad in which the speaker interrogates the north wind and receives three replies in quotation marks. The wind's final answer is a tricolon: 'Passionate tears for flowers departed: / Vain wrath, remorse, and icy grief' (ll. 22–24) — three kinds of spiritual weather, enumerated rather than amplified. Three ballad quatrains rhymed abcb; the speaker's lines are unquoted, the wind's in quotation marks throughout.
