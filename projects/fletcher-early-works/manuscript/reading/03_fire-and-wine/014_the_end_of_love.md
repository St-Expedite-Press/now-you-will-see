## Original Lineation

O, I am weary of Love,
  Of Love with the broken wings,
Of Love with the eyelids bound:
  Whose temple is shut and still,
  Alone on a hidden hill,
Far above any sound.

There is a little pool,
And the trailing leaf-sprays cool
  Dip into it, all day.
It whispers to me of rest,
And a slumbering Naiad's breast:
  There will I sink, and stay.

## Context Notes

Two six-line stanzas. ll. 2–3 ('Love with the broken wings,' 'eyelids bound'): the blindfolded Eros is a medieval and Renaissance convention signifying love's irrationality; the 'broken wings' intensify the exhaustion.

l. 11 ('a slumbering Naiad's breast'): the water-nymph of Greek myth inhabiting pools offers the speaker a pastoral alternative to love — 'There will I sink, and stay' (l. 12), a dissolution of desire into cool sleep.
