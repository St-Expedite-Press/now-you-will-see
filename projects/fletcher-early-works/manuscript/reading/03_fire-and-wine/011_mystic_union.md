## Original Lineation

I taste the sultry beauty of your mouth:
  'Tis like a scarlet poppy in the sun.
  Its sleepy passion has my strength undone,
And I am lost in vague dreams of the South.

The scented soft dark tangle of your hair
  Is like the shade of sighing cypress thrown
  On marble terraces where, one by one,
Gold glints are aimlessly drifting, here and there.

Yet in my soul I feel a vague disquiet:
  A lofty melancholy in the sun.
Stilled are my heartbeats, stilled my pulses' riot,
  I dream. Are we apart or are we one?

## Context Notes

Petrarchan sonnet (ABBA ABBA CDC DCC). The octave's two comparisons — mouth as 'a scarlet poppy in the sun' (l. 2) and hair as 'the shade of sighing cypress' (ll. 6–7) — place the poem in the landscape of Fletcher's Italian poems; the cypress is simultaneously a Mediterranean garden tree and a funerary one.

l. 12 ('Are we apart or are we one?'): the *unio mystica* question from the tradition of mystical love poetry (Song of Songs, Donne's 'The Ecstasy'), the moment at which bodily union reaches toward spiritual merger.
