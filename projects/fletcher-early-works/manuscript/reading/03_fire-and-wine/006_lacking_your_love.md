## Original Lineation

Lacking your love, my heart is a darkened house:
The dust lies thick on the carpetless, creaking floors:
The walls are covered with green mould: a cricket
  ironically chirps;
And the shattered windows let in the long autumn
  rains.

O grant me your love, press kiss upon kiss on my
  lips,
Till I can forget the dark ruin and havoc that time
  has made;
Till aswoon in your arms, my empty heart dreams of
  bright halls,
Lit up with curious lamps, and with dancers awhirl.

## Editorial Relineation

Lacking your love, my heart is a darkened house:
The dust lies thick on the carpetless, creaking floors:
The walls are covered with green mould: a cricket ironically chirps;
And the shattered windows let in the long autumn rains.

O grant me your love, press kiss upon kiss on my lips,
Till I can forget the dark ruin and havoc that time has made;
Till aswoon in your arms, my empty heart dreams of bright halls,
Lit up with curious lamps, and with dancers awhirl.

## Context Notes

Two quatrains. l. 1 ('Lacking your love, my heart is a darkened house'): the central conceit. The specifics of the ruin — 'carpetless, creaking floors,' 'green mould,' 'cricket ironically chirps,' 'shattered windows' (ll. 2–4) — transform the conventional Romantic metaphor into something closer to a Hardy or Dickens house of fallen gentility.

ll. 7–8: 'curious lamps' and 'dancers awhirl' contrast the ruin with an Edwardian drawing-room warmth imagined in the beloved's absence.
