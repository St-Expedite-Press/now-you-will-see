## Original Lineation

These are the clouds which receive our
    birth and death:
These are the clouds which drink our hope
    and despair;
And for ever, and for ever, long as the earth has
    breath,
The clouds shall melt away, leaving the wide sky
    bare.
These are the clouds that, when we see them aloft
    in the sky,
We no longer fear the sun's fierce monotony,
Nor the bronze deserts of ennui, where sudden
    spasms of lust
Melt in the slumbrous starkness, like thin phantoms
    of dust:--
God has looked on the earth and has seen but the
    earth's grey shrouds,
Whirling or clashing shapes, fiery or frozen clouds.
Clouds we are, and as clouds we are drifted away,
Rising from valleys of night to vanish before the
    day.

These are the clouds that, like female desires,
Wrap in their pallid longing the stern red peak:
These are the clouds, that like the male's dumb fires,
The gulf of the void forever and ever seek.
These are the clouds, that are weaving aloft in the
    sky
Shadow and light, rain, sun and mystery,
Earth's garments of revel, and earth's great shape-
    less shrouds:
For out of one cloud, at the last, have come all these
    clouds.
God unspeakable, God in whose hands life and death
Are as lightning and thunder wedded, fills them with
    breath:
As a Cloud He is, as a Cloud that cannot stay,
A pillar of fire by night, a shadowed shrine through
    the day.

## Editorial Relineation

These are the clouds which receive our birth and death:
These are the clouds which drink our hope and despair;
And for ever, and for ever, long as the earth has breath,
The clouds shall melt away, leaving the wide sky bare.
These are the clouds that, when we see them aloft in the sky,

Nor the bronze deserts of ennui, where sudden spasms of lust

God has looked on the earth and has seen but the earth's grey shrouds,
Whirling or clashing shapes, fiery or frozen clouds.
Clouds we are, and as clouds we are drifted away,

These are the clouds that, like female desires,
Wrap in their pallid longing the stern red peak:

These are the clouds, that are weaving aloft in the sky

A pillar of fire by night, a shadowed shrine through the day.

## Context Notes

Two twelve-line stanzas in loose hexameter couplets.

ll. 23–24 ('A pillar of fire by night, a shadowed shrine through the day'): The closing image is drawn from Exodus 13:21–22, where God leads Israel through the wilderness as a pillar of cloud by day and of fire by night.

ll. 13–16 ('the clouds that, like female desires, / Wrap in their pallid longing the stern red peak: ... like the male's dumb fires, / The gulf of the void forever and ever seek'): Cloud is given a sexual polarity — desire wrapping the peak (female), fires seeking the void (male) — framing cosmic process as an erotic dynamic.
