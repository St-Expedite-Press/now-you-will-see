## Original Lineation

All is night and nothing changes--what to
    me are grief and gladness?
All of life that is of a moment is all of
    the infinite universal;
And all of that all is but an outcry, a flicker of
    passing torches,
A jingle, a step, a rustle, on the bourne of unbounded
    darkness:
Seen by me the dreamer, as I dreamed of myself in a
    dream.
For I, too, am but a shade, a phantom that man has
    created,
I am unreal and real, and man who makes me of his
    substance,
Owns no purpose but to be made anew by me in my
    turn.
            In the never-ending darkness,
            From the emptiness, through the emptiness
                to the emptiness,
            Which life itself creates, through which it
                goes and returns.

## Editorial Relineation

All is night and nothing changes--what to me are grief and gladness?
All of life that is of a moment is all of the infinite universal;
And all of that all is but an outcry, a flicker of passing torches,
A jingle, a step, a rustle, on the bourne of unbounded darkness:
Seen by me the dreamer, as I dreamed of myself in a dream.
For I, too, am but a shade, a phantom that man has created,
I am unreal and real, and man who makes me of his substance,
Owns no purpose but to be made anew by me in my turn.
            In the never-ending darkness,
            From the emptiness, through the emptiness to the emptiness,
            Which life itself creates, through which it goes and returns.

## Context Notes

Eight lines of irregular free verse followed by a three-line indented tricolon set apart from the body of the poem.

l. 4 ('on the bourne of unbounded darkness'): 'Bourne' from French *borne*, meaning boundary or limit; cf. *Hamlet* III.i ('the undiscovered country from whose bourne / No traveller returns').

ll. 9–11 ('From the emptiness, through the emptiness to the emptiness'): The indented closing tricolon is a formulation close to the Buddhist *śūnyatā* (emptiness) doctrine; the repetition enacts circularity rather than progression.
