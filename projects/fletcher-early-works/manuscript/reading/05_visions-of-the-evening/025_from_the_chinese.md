## Original Lineation

The lanterns dangle at the end of long wires,
The breeze bobs them to and fro.
My soul is in love with that lazy lantern-dance.
Oh, how the autumn gusts through the dark gardens
Rattle them together, rending their crimson sides!

## Context Notes

Five lines in a single free-verse stanza; like poem 024, not a translation but a free Orientalist imitation — no Chinese source is identified.

ll. 4–5 ('the autumn gusts through the dark gardens / Rattle them together, rending their crimson sides'): The autumn storm destroys the festive lanterns without commentary, completing the poem's compressed arc from beauty to ruin.
