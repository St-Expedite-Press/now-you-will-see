## Original Lineation

I have climbed alone
    Till the gulf below is black:
There is no turning back,
I must needs go on.

Nor do I care if I
Ever the top attain:
Climbing gives no pain,
At the top I shall die.

If I stare upon the snow
Till I am blinded--Well,
It is better in Hell
To go as blind men go.

## Context Notes

Three quatrains in ABBA (envelope rhyme).

ll. 21–23 ('If I stare upon the snow / Till I am blinded--Well, / It is better in Hell / To go as blind men go'): Snow-blindness (corneal UV burn from snow reflection) is a precisely observed consequence of mountain ascent; its acceptance here as preferable to turning back is the poem's central statement.
