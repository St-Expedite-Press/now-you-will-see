## Original Lineation

Be sensual, be mysterious, O my Night:
Let not the day end in a blaze of light,
But overwhelm it in your ashy flood
Slowly, as dying soldier's dripping blood.
Be sensual, be mysterious, O my Night.

Be sensual, be mysterious, O my Night:
Impress me heavily with your flaccid might.
Let there be murmurs, jangled shrieks in the air,
Raving madness, and voluptuous despair!
Be sensual, be mysterious, O my Night.

Be sensual, be mysterious, O my Night:
Crawl over the sky in your vast shell void of light,
Old tortoise, for such maniacs as I,
Who never have learned to live and cannot die.
Be sensual, be mysterious, O my Night.

## Context Notes

Three five-line stanzas, each opening and closing with the refrain 'Be sensual, be mysterious, O my Night,' in the litanic mode of Baudelaire's *Litanies de Satan*.

ll. 23–25 ('Crawl over the sky in your vast shell void of light, / Old tortoise, for such maniacs as I, / Who never have learned to live and cannot die'): Night figured as a tortoise with the sky as its shell; the speaker identifies as one who is neither properly alive nor able to die.
