## Original Lineation

'Gainst the sorrows of to-day,
    I have grimly striven to fight,
Waiting for the summons gay
    Of the lamplit streets to-night.

Arches festooned over dark
    Led me onwards to the light:
'Twas a dull and chilly spark
    Closed within a cage of night.

Better was the day but past:
    Then I failed but strove in might:
Now I bind my own strength fast
    To illusions of the night.

'Tis a mockery to sleep,
    And a weariness to fight.
Let the dark days hide me deep
    In the all-encompassing night.

## Context Notes

Four quatrains in ABAB, the rhyme-word 'night' closes every even line across all four stanzas, functioning as an unannounced refrain.

ll. 15–16 ('Arches festooned over dark / Led me onwards to the light'): Decorated lamp-arch spans were used in London for civic festive lighting; 'arches festooned' suggests garlands or chains of lights rather than a permanent structure.

ll. 17–18 ('Twas a dull and chilly spark / Closed within a cage of night'): The irony named in the title: night, sought as refuge, proves as confining as the day it was meant to relieve.
