## Original Lineation

The City lies at ease upon the night:
Her bed is hung with jewels--glorious one!
And all her palaces are but a throne
Of splendour, sparkling in the joy of light.

And over all the region of the spheres
There booms her song of night and light and fire,
Her fierce tempestuous challenge of desire,
That fails not, nor shall fail in future years.

Cry that the night hath hid thee not, and still
Proclaim in strident clarions all thy might;
Let laughing tumult leap from hill to hill:
Thou blessed one, my City of the night!

## Context Notes

A twelve-line lyric of pure urban celebration -- unusual in *The Dominant City* for its unambiguous praise: 'Thou blessed one, my City of the night!' The city here is both lover and monarch, 'her bed hung with jewels' (the electric lights), her 'palaces a throne of splendour.' The 'region of the spheres' is the classical cosmological model of the Ptolemaic heavens (crystalline spheres bearing the planets, beyond which lay the fixed stars), applied ironically to London's skies: the city's electric lights rival the celestial order. 'Strident clarions' are loudly sounding trumpet-calls (a clarion is a medieval trumpet with a clear, shrill sound), here applied to the city's ongoing declaration of its own existence.
