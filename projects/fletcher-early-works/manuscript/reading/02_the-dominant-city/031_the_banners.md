## Original Lineation

Like ruddy or tawny masses of torn flame,
Over the whirlpool seething in agony
Defiantly they flap and shake on high
The electricity of life, that, ever the same,
Fulminates in the city's pain and shame,
And streams in smoke-clouds towards the ashen
  sky;
A roaring chaos of wrath and mystery
Fashioned to pleasure That-Which-Has-No
  Name:
Banners on banners heavily everywhere,
Soul-oriflammes of blood and hate and lust,
Burst flickering through the abysms of the air.
Leap, condors chained; it is our will; you must.
And scream our tragedy even to those dim
Veils of the dawn, where red stars flicker grim!

## Context Notes

The source prints the sonnet solid; the editorial text divides it at l. 9 ('Banners on banners heavily everywhere'), where the sestet announces itself and gathers the octave's single flame-image into its final heap. An oriflamme (l. 10, 'Soul-oriflammes') was the red silk battle-banner of the kings of France, here applied to the banners of blood, hate, and lust streaming across the city sky. Sonnet, octave abbaabba, sestet cdcdee.
