## Original Lineation

Undying sun of the years, pour forth thy blood
Till men grow drunken with the strength thereof:
Let us be wrapped in flame as in a hood.
And perish and be reborn in fiery love.

Sun that grows pale each winter, through the bold
Death-struggle of desire we recreate
On the worn anvils of will, your power old
To be our song, our glory, and our fate.

## Context Notes

Ll. 5–8 ('Sun that grows pale each winter, through the bold / Death-struggle of desire we recreate / On the worn anvils of will, your power old / To be our song, our glory, and our fate') — the 'worn anvils of will' invoke the forge tradition of Hephaestus/Vulcan, the Greek and Roman gods of smithcraft, applying it to human creative energy as a cosmically productive force. Two pentameter quatrains, abab.
