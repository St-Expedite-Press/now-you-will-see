Death-agonies, dances, resurrections hurled,
Have made for me, and made for you, a world:
With the red lava-torrents of the suns
Drunken, we dance yet to the rain's dark drums,
And watch the universe, life, birth, and death,
Hanging each instant on a single breath.

## Context Notes

Form: six iambic pentameters in couplets, 'suns/drums' slant.

The dedication is an allegiance: the living French poets of 1913 are the line this book proceeds to borrow from -- Verhaeren's industrial visions above all, with Baudelaire standing behind the whole enterprise. 'Drunken, we dance yet to the rain's dark drums' announces the programme: ecstasy taken from weather and machinery, not from the Muses.
