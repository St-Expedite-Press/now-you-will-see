## Original Lineation

Light is horror and darkness beauty--only in the
  vague hours where they mingle,
Can all who wander without the great dark pale
  enclosing
That intangible unreal loveliness, catch some faint
  apprehension
Of what the power of Night, without beginning or
  end in the seasons,
Without change, limitless in space, may be to the
  Gods unknown
Contemplating in its perfect constancy their own
  unconsciousness.

## Editorial Relineation

Light is horror and darkness beauty--only in the vague hours where they mingle,
Can all who wander without the great dark pale enclosing
That intangible unreal loveliness, catch some faint apprehension
Of what the power of Night, without beginning or end in the seasons,
Without change, limitless in space, may be to the
  Gods unknown
Contemplating in its perfect constancy their own unconsciousness.

## Context Notes

'The great dark pale' (l. 2): the *pale* in English can mean a fence-post or boundary stake, hence 'beyond the pale' = outside the limit; here, darkness is the enclosing boundary of all apprehensible form. The closing lines (ll. 5–6) — 'the power of Night... may be to the Gods unknown / Contemplating in its perfect constancy their own unconsciousness' — propose the gods' self-unconscious contemplation within limitless night as the ultimate condition of being: a Schopenhauerian rather than Christian formulation.

A single sentence in six unrhymed long lines.
