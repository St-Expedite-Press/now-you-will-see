## Original Lineation

I've seen all to be seen,
Have known all to be known.
And everywhere I've been;
From the pit up to the throne.

In strength I reared my stones;
Life idly knocks them down:--
To shape, as it did once,
Another god--or clown?

Faded that dream, to be:
The past is burned away;
I leap into the sea . . .
It takes me.  Life was gay.

## Context Notes

Three cross-rhyme quatrains, ABAB throughout. l. 24 ('Life was gay'): 'gay' in the early twentieth-century sense of cheerful, carefree — the past tense signals completed retrospect.
