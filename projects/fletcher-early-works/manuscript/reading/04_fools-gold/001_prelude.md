## Original Lineation

Let me have strange new song so that my soul
May not grow sad and cold
By the ashes of the old;
Let me have strange new song to rhyme and roll.

Let it be fair or base,
Even as this life is;
Commingled woe and bliss,
Changing as change the pulses' steady race.

Enough for me to sing,
And gather with my sheaves
Dead roots and bitter leaves;
Let others know the joy--if joy it is--
That fame can bring.

Let me have strange new song:
On with the rhythm of life,
I leap nor fear the strife;
My soul is dancing, dancing, all day long!

## Context Notes

Four envelope-rhyme quatrains (ABBA) with an anaphoric refrain: the opening phrase 'Let me have strange new song' (l. 1) returns at l. 27, giving the poem a modified rondeau frame. ll. 22–23 ('And gather with my sheaves / Dead roots and bitter leaves') pair the harvest image with the collection's title figure.
