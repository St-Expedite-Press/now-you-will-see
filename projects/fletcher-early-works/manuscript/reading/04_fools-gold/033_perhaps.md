## Original Lineation

Some day the goal we seek
Will crown this quest of ours,
We will creep back, worn and weak,
From the desert to the flowers.

We will ask no aim of life,
And so will gain a time
Of peace apart from strife,
And joy, and joyous rhyme.

And we will never know
How happy we are, there:
How time moves fast or slow,
How foulness makes life fair.

For we will merely--play!
And sweep away from sight
The riddles of the day
And the answers of the night.

## Context Notes

Four cross-rhyme quatrains, ABAB throughout. The poem is notably in the first-person plural ('we'), unusual for this collection; no antecedent is given.
