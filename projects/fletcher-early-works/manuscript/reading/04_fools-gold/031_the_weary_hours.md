## Original Lineation

Now I have had my day,
And all my songs are sung:
A mirthless crew were they,
With never a laughing tongue.

Still pass the hours' swift feet;
But not a note they raise,
Nor chorus bitter-sweet
For winter's greyest days.

My hours, why cease from talk?
See you some dawn aglow?
"No: but we yet must walk
Or we would faint with woe."

## Context Notes

Three ballad quatrains, ABCB throughout. The third stanza turns to direct dialogue: ll. 23–24 are the personified hours' quoted reply.
