## Original Lineation

Life mocks at me and I mock back at life:
Life sobs and I repeat its cries of pain,
Life rages and I swell with empty strife,
Life revels and I turn to dance again.
But wherefore should I echo joy and pain
And dangle at the puppet strings of strife,
Why ring the changes on old tunes again
To satisfy that weary tyrant, Life?

No, I will either break my lyre, or fill
My verses with disgust and scorn of days;
Yet hold the idle dream in secret still
Of song too deep for rhyme, too high for praise:
So that, if rarely, I may feel the thrill
Of joy eternal and the deathless bays.

## Context Notes

Fourteen lines rhyming ABABABABCDCDEE — a sonnet with an elongated octet (eight lines) followed by a sestet. l. 25 ('deathless bays'): the laurel (bay) wreath, classical emblem of poetic immortality.
