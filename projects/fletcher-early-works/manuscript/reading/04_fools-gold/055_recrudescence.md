## Original Lineation

Midwinter grim and grey
What do you bring me, pray?
"What, but a darker day,
A stormier night,
A bleak sleet-driven way,
No inn in sight!"

Then, winter, cease your shock:
I am no granite rock
To bear the strain.
"Although you cannot fight,
I'll leave you some small might
Until I come again."

Well, winter, start your task.
Be merciless: I ask
Only so much of you.
"I'll flay your soul, no fear,
So that the coming year
Must make you all anew."

## Context Notes

Three six-line stanzas; each stanza alternates the speaker's address with winter's quoted reply (ll. 13–16, 21–23, 28–30). The title uses a medical term: 'recrudescence' — the renewed outbreak of a disease or condition after apparent improvement.
