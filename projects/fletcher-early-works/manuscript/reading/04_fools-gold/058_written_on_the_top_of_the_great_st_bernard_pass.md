## Original Lineation

Multitudes northward and southward, traverse
        the dreary grey pass;
Carrying over invisibly burdens of sorrow and
        care.
They meet, and they look at each other: they pass,
        and they never return:
The road remains smooth and the mountains
        frozen and bare.

## Editorial Relineation

Multitudes northward and southward, traverse the dreary grey pass;
Carrying over invisibly burdens of sorrow and care.
They meet, and they look at each other: they pass, and they never return:
The road remains smooth and the mountains frozen and bare.

## Context Notes

Four long lines in a single quatrain; ll. 11–18 are set as turnover lines in the source, restored as four full lines in the editorial text. The Great St. Bernard Pass is a high Alpine transalpine route (2,469 m) on the Swiss–Italian border; the Hospice at the summit was founded c. 1050 by Bernard of Aosta.
