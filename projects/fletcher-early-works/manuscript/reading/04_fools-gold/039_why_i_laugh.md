## Original Lineation

Through England howling, late and soon,
Reformers bellow for the moon,
To me it seems small mystery;
I like her better in the sky.

Stumbling through ditches of the mind
They wander on, blind leading blind:
Sinners themselves, they yet must be
Picked guardians of morality.

I do not keep my brother like
Some men I know, who always strike
Upon your faults, yet stay content
To be even as they were sent.

I do not prettify my life,
Ere I dare look her in the face:
The jade! she makes a proper wife;
Methinks to love is no disgrace.

## Context Notes

Four cross-rhyme quatrains, ABAB throughout. l. 17 ('blind leading blind') echoes Matthew 15:14. l. 28 ('jade'): archaic for a disreputable woman, used here as a figure for Life.
