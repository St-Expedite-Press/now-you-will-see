## Original Lineation

What matter if we live life full and just,
Taste grief but little ere we pass to dust:
Or if we use each moment we have known
On death to contemplate, for death to groan?
From death to life and back to death we fly,
Does any one count the insects when they die?

## Context Notes

Six lines in three rhyming couplets (AA BB CC). The poem is an unargued aphorism in epigrammatic form.
