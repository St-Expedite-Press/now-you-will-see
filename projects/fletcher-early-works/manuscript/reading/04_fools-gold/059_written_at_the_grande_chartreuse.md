## Original Lineation

Happy are birds and beasts, water and trees.
They give us what they can of their own ease.
Unhappy alone is man.  Upon this spot
I dreamed that I found bliss, but found it not.

## Context Notes

Four lines in two rhyming couplets (AA BB). Companion inscription to the preceding poem (058). The Grande Chartreuse is the mother-house of the Carthusian order, founded by Saint Bruno in 1084 in the Chartreuse mountains north of Grenoble; l. 14 ('I dreamed that I found bliss, but found it not') echoes Matthew Arnold's 'Stanzas from the Grande Chartreuse' (1855), which meditates on lost faith at the same location.
