## Original Lineation

At last I sit beyond all joy and hope;
Beyond all fear and sorrow now I rest.

The sky is grey, evenly diffused the light,
There is no single shadow anywhere.

I am alone, and silence is about me:
I listen, listen, to that silence long.

And in its inmost depths I catch a murmur,
A pale, faint whisper, echoing evermore:

"All is now as it was, all that must follow
Must be ever the same, down to the end.

Chance, blundering, built on nothingness the
  mansions
Of life, which into nothingness must fall.

Then weary not your soul with passing sorrows
But hear the words of everlasting joy:

Your sense will sink in sweet annihilation,
Abide until that time of perfect peace."

## Context Notes

Seven unrhymed couplets in loose free verse, with a turn at l. 9 into quoted direct speech that occupies the remaining six couplets. ll. 27–28 contain a turnover line in the source; the editorial text restores the single line.
