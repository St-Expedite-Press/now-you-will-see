## Original Lineation

I shudder, and am revolted at the thought
Of this vile body, from which there is no escape:
Leprous with sin, corrupt and putrefying,
Stained, broken, with the efforts I have made
To outdo folly with folly, and to stand
Over all ages, a vast mockery.

Could I but fly from out this cage of flesh,
This instant would I do it: even death
Rejects my plea and with disgust turns back,
From such a carrion-prey: and for my songs,
They are so evil that Shame smiles at them.

There is naught left, then, but to hide myself,
Content, in the deaf desert of my Pride,
And worship there my image which is cracked
From crown to base.  So Thy last punishment
O God--or Devil--is, I cherish Hell!

## Context Notes

Fifteen lines in three irregular free-verse verse paragraphs. l. 28 ('O God--or Devil--is, I cherish Hell!'): the ambiguity of address — 'God--or Devil' — is in the source text; retained as copy-text.
