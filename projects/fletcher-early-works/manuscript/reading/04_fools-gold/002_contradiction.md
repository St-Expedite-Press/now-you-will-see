## Original Lineation

The earth holds in its being day and night,
And, some think, there are three:
Light, darkness, which we see,
And one more thing behind which keeps from
  sight.

Seeking, I found not: dreaming, could not know:
But through me runs the flow--
Like two great separate seas--
Day, darkness, with their storms and ecstacies.

Therefore look not for wisdom in these lines,
Nor combat with some wrong,
For he needs not a song
Nor day, nor night, nor earth, who wisdom finds.

I sing both good and bad: let contradiction share!
If there be truth behind
I do not greatly care;
At any rate, I know it is not in my mind.

The earth's to choose from: what man could
  refrain?
Let me praise day and night
Or hate them both as blight,
But let me strike the lyre once and again!

## Context Notes

Five envelope-rhyme and cross-rhyme quatrains (ABBA / ABAB). l. 8 ('Day, darkness, with their storms and ecstacies'): 'ecstacies' is the source printing's spelling, retained as copy-text. ll. 32–33 ('The earth's to choose from: what man could / refrain?') are set as a turnover line in the source; the editorial text restores the single line.
